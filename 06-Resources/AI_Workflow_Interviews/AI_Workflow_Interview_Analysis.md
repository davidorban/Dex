# AI Workflow Interview Analysis — Cross-Cutting Findings

**Scope:** 6 interviews (Veron Shim, Luke Nesci, Miray Zaki, Ana Capuder, Nate Kelleher, Jerry Brooks/Jad Jubaili)
**Date:** March 2026
**Author:** David Orban

## Executive Summary

Six interviews reveal a consistent picture: a high-talent, fast-moving organization where people spend disproportionate time on formatting, triaging, and coordinating rather than strategic and relationship work. The gap between what people are hired to do and what they actually spend their days doing represents the clearest AI opportunity.

**Key findings:**
- **Presentation creation is the universal bottleneck** — 4 of 6 interviewees independently identified it as top friction
- **Team is unusually AI-ready** — 3 building own AI tools, 2 use AI daily, 1 substantively skeptical
- **5 of 6 top opportunities score 15+/18** — frequent, time-consuming, repetitive, already digital
- **High-judgment work remains human** — clean separation between automatable and strategic tasks
- **Accessibility as force multiplier** — Jerry's ADD/dyslexia makes AI summarization transformative, not incremental
