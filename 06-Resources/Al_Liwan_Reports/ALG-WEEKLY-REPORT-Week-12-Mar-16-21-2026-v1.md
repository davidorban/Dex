### AI Workflow Interview Analysis — Key Q1 Deliverable

The **AI Workflow Interview Analysis** document was completed this week, synthesizing six "Workflows for AI" interviews conducted in Week 11 into a comprehensive cross-cutting findings report with an actionable implementation roadmap. This is the primary R3 deliverable for Q1 — translating the AI-Native strategy from direction-setting into a concrete, user-mapped, timeline-bound execution plan.

**Scope:** 6 interviews across Wealth Management (Veron), Strategy (Luke Nesci), CIO Office (Miray Zaki), CEO Office (Ana Capuder), Investment Banking UK (Nate Kelleher), and Business Development (Jerry Brooks). The analysis identified 18 distinct AI opportunities, scored each on frequency, time cost, repetitiveness, data availability, quality sensitivity, and AI tool readiness.

**Key findings:**

- **Presentation creation is the universal bottleneck** — 4 of 6 interviewees independently identified it as their top friction. Luke can spend days on a single deck; Veron describes formatting as his primary frustration; Nate needs presentation support for deal packaging; Jerry needs decks summarized. This convergence makes the branded presentation pipeline the single highest-leverage investment.
- **The team is unusually AI-ready** — 3 members already building their own AI tools (Nate's custom RAG engine, Luke's Claude Code experiments with knowledge graphs), 2 use AI daily (Ana with Perplexity/Claude, Luke with Cowork), only 1 (Miray) is substantively skeptical. The adoption challenge is tooling and integration, not awareness.
- **5 of 6 top opportunities score 15+/18** — an unusually strong signal that pain points are frequent, time-consuming, repetitive, and already digital — the ideal profile for AI automation.
- **High-judgment work remains human** — clean separation between automatable tasks (formatting, triaging, scheduling) and strategic work (deal assessment, relationship management, content expertise), meaning implementation carries low organizational risk.
- **Accessibility as force multiplier** — Jerry Brooks's ADD and dyslexia make AI document summarization transformative rather than incremental; it removes a functional obstacle to deal evaluation.

**5 Strategic Implementation Tracks:**

1. **Branded Presentation Pipeline** (Track 1) — Users: Veron, Luke, Nate, Jerry. Tool: Claude Cowork + pptx skill + Al Liwan brand guidelines. Timeline: Week 1 template + live demo. Success metric: creation time from days/hours to under 30 minutes.
2. **Executive Intelligence Layer** (Track 2) — Users: Ana (primary), Luke. Tool: Claude Cowork + Outlook MCP + web search. Automated meeting prep, action item extraction, multi-calendar coordination. Prerequisite: swap Ana's ARM laptop for Intel.
3. **Deal Flow Intelligence** (Track 3) — Users: Nate (primary), Jerry, Miray (post-mandate). Tool: Claude Code for RAG migration, Cowork for deck analysis. Migrate Nate's OpenAI RAG engine to corporate Claude; pitch deck analysis workflow; proactive opportunity surfacing agent.
4. **Operational Documentation** (Track 4) — Users: Miray (primary), Luke. Tool: Claude Cowork + docx skill. SLA/SOP templates, swim-lane workflow documentation, investment committee process docs.
5. **Relationship & Contact Management** (Track 5) — Users: Jerry (primary), Veron, Ana. Tool: Claude Cowork + CRM/Pipedrive integration. Automated CRM enrichment, follow-up tracking, contact network intelligence.

**AI Readiness Matrix:** Luke Nesci (Advanced, Champion — Claude Code), Nate Kelleher (Advanced, Champion — Claude Code), Ana Capuder (Advanced, Champion — Cowork), Veron (Basic, Medium — Cowork), Jerry Brooks (Basic, Medium — Cowork), Miray Zaki (Moderate, Low — Copilot May 2026).

**Implementation timeline:** Weeks 1-2 quick wins (branded template, live demos, RAG migration, Ana laptop swap); Month 1 foundation layer (content templates, deal surfacing agent, CRM enrichment, SLA/SOP templates); Months 2-3 deeper integration (multi-calendar coordination, knowledge graph, WhatsApp Business); Month 3+ enterprise integration (Microsoft Copilot + Claude May 2026, on-premise models).

**Concerns and mitigations addressed:** Data confidentiality (Azure UAE data residency), AI research accuracy (position for workflow automation not primary research), AI output quality (focus on analysis/formatting not relationship communication), hardware blocker (Ana's ARM laptop swap), adoption sustainability (champion network + periodic check-ins).
