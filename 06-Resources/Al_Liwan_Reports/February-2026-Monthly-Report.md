## Key Achievements and Milestones

### 1. Head of Technology Recruitment and Transition Planning

The most significant organisational development of February was the recruitment and initial briefing of Samson Giwa as Al Liwan's incoming Head of Technology. The process moved from job description adaptation (Week 07), through candidate review of three finalists — Samson, Adam, and Alexei (Week 08), to Samson's selection and a landmark handover briefing on 26 February (Week 09).

The "Intro to Al Liwan Tech" briefing covered 25 topics including: the Group's divisional IT needs, the IT team and support model, identity and cloud infrastructure (M365/Azure/Entra ID), security posture and gaps, network and hardware, cybersecurity and business continuity, the software landscape ($301K/yr), AI strategy and tools, the enterprise systems roadmap ($1.559M H2), people processes, communication tools, active projects, 14 published IT policies, the H1 roadmap (27 items), risks, budget scenarios (Conservative $376K / Baseline $2.36M / Growth $2.86M), inherited strengths and gaps, and 8 immediate priorities for Samson's first 30 days.

Key decisions emerging from the transition: MSSP and pen test vendor procurement explicitly paused pending Samson's assessment; major provisioning decisions deferred until his tenure; Jerry Brooks to meet Samson during his Abu Dhabi visit in early March; Amina confirmed Samson will attend pre-start meetings to accelerate his understanding.

### 2. IT Governance Baseline Established

February saw the completion of the core governance documentation package that had been under development since the mandate began:

- **IT Architecture Landscape v1.0** — Authored by Cigdem Kemi, elevated to v1.0 through AI-assisted review. Documents key architecture assumptions: M365 core platform, Azure AD/Entra ID identity, Intune endpoint management, Tresorit file sharing, pending CRM/ERP selections, SWIFT connectivity requirement, and multi-region architecture (UAE primary with UK/KSA/Switzerland planned). Includes a 10-entry decision register.
- **IT Governance Framework v1.0** — Compiled as a single governance wrapper incorporating the JML process, RBAC baseline, CAB-lite change control, vendor management framework, and data classification policy.
- **Service Catalogue v2.0** — Comprehensive catalogue documenting all IT services with service levels, owners, and request procedures.
- **BC/DR/IR Plan v1.0** — Business Continuity, Disaster Recovery, and Incident Response plan drafted as first version.
- **Technology & Innovation Binder H1 2026** — Board-grade compilation physically delivered to Michael Gale and Amina Olupitan, containing the IT roadmap (Gantt view and narrative), interim governance framework, full policy set (14 policies), IT handbook, role charter, and operating glossary.

Additional governance achievements: three-layer governance model (policy, process, SOPs) designed; daily standup cadence established with 15-minute hard stop; email signature governance researched and CodeTwo recommended; document naming convention formalised (ALG-IT-[TYPE]-[DATE]-v[VERSION]).

### 3. AI-Native Programme Launch and Expansion

The AI Knowledge Hour programme was the defining innovation initiative of February, delivering four consecutive weekly sessions that transformed AI from an abstract strategy into an operational capability across the organisation:

- **Week 06 — Inaugural AI Knowledge Hour**: Tool selection, licensing, automation workflows (transcript-to-Asana), CRM data analysis, Claude demonstrations, financial modelling, shared prompt/template repository creation
- **Week 07 — AI Knowledge Hour #2 Advanced**: Conversation export and classification, file system integration, Excel plugins, skills building and distribution, LinkedIn data extraction, delegation mindset, burnout and wellbeing
- **Week 07 — IT Tools: Intro to Asana**: Tasks, projects, portfolios, three view types, collaboration features, approval workflows, voice dictation demonstration (Whisper Flow to Claude to Asana)
- **Week 08 — AI Knowledge Hour #3: Introduction to Claude**: Claude capabilities overview, Chat vs Cowork modes, hands-on exercises, workflow integration, practical use cases. Workshop materials (PPTX and DOCX) produced as reusable training assets.

The Ali1 Constellation agent naming convention was culturally cleared by Shaheen AlShanableh and Randy Boyer, establishing Arabic-origin star names (Nashira, Altair, Suhail, Ankaa, Aldebaran, etc.) as employee agent namespaces. A registry of 31 star names supports 310+ agents at current capacity, designed to scale using the full Arabic astronomical tradition.

AI pilot use cases advanced across multiple fronts: ALPHA10X decision-intelligence platform moved to NDA stage; AI agent management track demonstrated real outputs through Axel Zanner Entwistle's financial modelling and market research; Claude enterprise deployment expanded (Luke Nesci and Camille Isuan granted access); OpenClaw agent evaluation commenced with 5 instances; MeetGeek enterprise evaluation initiated; and the NASHIRA persistent memory architecture was designed.

### 4. IT Budget and Financial Governance

The board approved a $650K IT budget for 2026 (excluding New Enterprise Systems). Jerry Brooks clarified the budget excludes second-half-year items pending revenue triggers. David proposed a rolling quarterly review cadence: each session summarises the prior quarter and agrees priorities for the following two.

The review concluded that the H1 budget is provisionally approved in scale though not in specific actions; H2 enterprise systems budget ($1.559M) remains gated on revenue; and major provisioning and MSSP decisions are deferred until Samson is onboard.
